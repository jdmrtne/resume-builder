ATS Optimization Guide

Beat the bots and get your resume in front of humans

Use Keywords from the Job Description

ATS systems scan for exact keywords. Mirror the language in the job posting — if they say "project management" don't write "managing projects." Use the Keywords tab in the builder to see what's missing.

Keep Formatting Simple

Avoid tables, headers/footers, text boxes, and images. ATS parsers struggle with complex layouts. Stick to standard section headings like Work Experience, Education, and Skills. Use the Classic ATS or Swiss Minimal templates.

Quantify Your Achievements

Replace vague descriptions with measurable results. Instead of "improved sales," write "increased sales by 35% in Q3 2024." Numbers stand out to both ATS and human reviewers. Use the AI ✨ tab to generate metric-rich bullets.

Avoid Common ATS Killers

  • ❌ Don't use headers/footers for contact info — parsers often skip them
  • ❌ Don't submit as .docx if PDF is accepted — PDFs parse more reliably
  • ❌ Don't use icons or graphics in place of text
  • ❌ Don't abbreviate job titles — write "Software Engineer" not "SWE"
  • ❌ Don't use generic file names — name it FirstName-LastName-Resume.pdf

Tailor for Every Application

A one-size-fits-all resume rarely beats ATS. Spend 10 minutes customizing your summary and skills for each role. Use the Keywords tab to paste the job description and instantly see which terms to add.
